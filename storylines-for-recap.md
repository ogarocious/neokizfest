## 🎯 The Master Arc

**The premise:** A festival ends. The organizer owes ~$34,000 back to 206 people. Instead of disappearing, he builds a refund system in public, runs it for 48+ days, and discovers that the community he built responds with something he didn't expect — grace at scale.

**The resolution (so far):** 39.6% of the entire liability was erased by the community itself — not by Charles paying it back, but by people voluntarily forgiving it. Nearly half of everyone who filed chose to waive. The math tells a story no one planned.

## 📖 Individual Storylines

### 1. Paige Charles — "I'm donating half even though I'm barely feeding myself"
**Type:** Partial refund, completed
**The story:** Paige bought a pass and never made it — a car accident with injuries caused physical and brain damage. She couldn't attend the festival, which was already postponed. She may never attend a dance festival again. She wanted to donate the full $1,200 she spent. But the accident has taken so much from her that she's barely feeding herself. So she split it: requested half back, donated half.
> *"I wish I could donate the entire $1,200 I spent on my pass. But also due to the amount that this accident has cost me I am also barely feeding myself right now. I'm donating half."*
**Why it matters for content:** The most powerful single act of generosity in the dataset — from someone who had every reason not to give.

---

### 2. Sara Griess — "NeoKiz was a literal life saver"
**Type:** Waived refund + donor
**The story:** Sara was deeply depressed and burnt out before NeoKiz 2023, "teetering on the edge of giving up entirely." She came to the festival and something shifted. At the pool party, the lyrics *"did you check on me, did you notice me"* played during what she describes as one of the safest, most caring dances of the weekend — with her "dance dad" Ceijay. She cried. She calls it a moment of release that saved her life.
> *"That moment of release saved me. None of this could've happened without the special vibe that Neokiz generated."*
She waived her refund AND donated additionally.
**Why it matters for content:** A moment at a pool party — not a headliner set, not a workshop — is what saved someone. The festival's culture, not its programming, was the product.

### 3. Tanisha Davis — "I believed in you then, and I still do"

## 📊 Data Storylines (for podcast/video narration)

### The 39.6% Story
Of every dollar Charles owed, the community erased 40 cents before he touched it. Not through legal action — through choice. 78 people clicked "waive." 38 people donated separately. 23 people accepted less than they were owed and said keep the rest.

### The Waive Rate Story
42.6% of everyone who filed chose to forgive. No ask preceded it. No incentive. The form gave them the option; nearly half took it. By the time the queue is closed, the rate among fully resolved cases is 46.4%.

### The Non-Filer Story
12 ticket holders — after 48 days, an open system, and $1,346.62 collectively owed to them — have never filed. Some may be waiting. Some may have quietly let it go. That $1,346 may never move.

### The 48-Day Arc
- **Day 1 (Feb 14):** Site launches. No one knows if it'll work.
- **Day 8:** First wave of submissions. Waive rate emerging.
- **Week 3:** Zelle hits first monthly limit. Pause. Community waits.
- **Day 37:** Zelle resumes. Payment burst clears the backlog.
- **Day 44:** Zelle hits limit again. Second pause. But now the queue is small.
- **Day 48:** 90 paid, 78 waived, 87.4% resolved. Net owed: $3,585.

### The $824 Story
After donations offset the remaining obligation ($3,585 − $2,925 in donations), the true out-of-pocket from here is **$824**. The community gave enough to nearly cover the finish line on its own.
